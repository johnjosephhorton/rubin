Job Design Visualizer

for “Chaining Tasks, Redefining Work: A Theory of AI Automation”

Two-stage cost-minimization: steps → tasks (AI strategy), then tasks → jobs (worker assignment).

Cost Parameters

Each step has a skill cost and a time cost for each mode of human (cMi, tMi) or AI-assisted (cAi, tAi) execution. These costs for AI-automated execution are zero. qi and tHi denote the AI's success probability and the (potential) hand-off time cost between workers.

The solver minimizes the firm's long-run cost-minimization problem, denoted in Equation 3 in the paper.

Stage 1AI Strategy: Steps → Tasks

Each step is executed as Manual, Automated, or Augmented (chains: one or more Automated steps followed by an Augmented step).

A Manual step becomes a manual task; consecutive Automated→Augmented steps form one AI chain task.

Stage 2Job Design: Tasks → Jobs

Given the task sequence from Stage 1, tasks are partitioned into jobs (one worker per job). Each job boundary incurs a hand-off cost determined by the final step of that job.
