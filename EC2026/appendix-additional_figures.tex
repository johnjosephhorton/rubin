  \vspace{0.4cm}
  \raggedright \footnotesize{\emph{Notes:} 
  This figure illustrates how a firm aggregates steps into tasks and tasks into jobs. 
  The top layer shows $m=7$ steps executed in manual (gray), automated (green), or augmented (orange) modes, with dashed boxes indicating task groupings. 
  The middle layer aggregates steps into $n=5$ tasks, classified as human-executed tasks (olive) or AI chains (purple), with cyan dashed boxes indicating job groupings. 
  The bottom layer aggregates tasks into $p=3$ jobs (cyan), each assigned to a separate worker. 
  Vertical colored arrows show aggregation across layers, and horizontal arrows show production flow within each layer. 
  Red curly arrows mark hand-off costs incurred at job boundaries, and can be traced from the bottom layer to the corresponding tasks and steps above. 
  }
