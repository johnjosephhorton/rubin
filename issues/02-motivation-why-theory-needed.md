# Issue 02 — Why is this theory needed? (motivation gap)

## Raised by
**R4 (first bullet).** Implicitly echoed by R2's "not earthshattering" remark.

> "What question can we answer or answer better by viewing the world through this lens? Where do existing theories fall short? What features of the data can the theory speak to but others cannot? Why do these data features plausibly matter?"
>
> "The current version of the paper, which starts talking about any data only on page 34, treats the empirical content of its theory, and to what extent it is backed up by data, as a bit of an afterthought."

## Verification against source
- `intro.tex` (617 lines) spends most of the runway defining steps/tasks/jobs conceptually. First data reference surfaces in `empirics.tex` only. Confirmed — R4's observation holds.
- `literature.tex` positions the paper against Autor-Thompson (2025) and Acemoglu but doesn't carve out a sharp "what existing theories can't do" statement up front.

## Options to address

1. **Lead with the empirical puzzle.** Open the intro with a stylized fact the theory uniquely explains: e.g. "AI-executed tasks cluster in contiguous sequences within occupations" + "exposure ≠ execution conditional on fragmentation". Frame the theory as needed to rationalize these. Would move current intro material into Section 2.
2. **Add a dedicated "what this theory delivers" subsection** (half page) in the intro that lists 3–4 data features existing task-based models cannot explain, with forward references to the empirical tests.
3. **Contrast with Autor-Thompson / Acemoglu explicitly.** A half-page table: phenomenon × "canonical task model" × "chaining model" × "matches data?". Would also cover R1.3's J-curve ambiguity.

| Option | Effort | Payoff |
|---|---|---|

**Recommendation:** Do Option 1 as the structural move, Option 3 as a supporting device inside the rewritten intro. This also attacks Issue 08 (streamline intro) and feeds directly into Issue 03 (reframe).
