# Issue 27 — Cite related task-based model of AI in the literature review

## Summary
A topically related task-based model of AI automation — **Hampole, Papanikolaou, Schmidt & Seegmiller, "Artificial Intelligence and the Labor Market," NBER Working Paper No. [33509](https://www.nber.org/papers/w33509)** (2025) — should be cited alongside the other task-based models referenced in the literature review. Core thesis: if automation is concentrated in a few tasks and tasks are complements, labor demand can increase.

The mechanism is distinct from this paper's chaining story (their complementarity operates through the production function, not through validation-checkpoint sharing inside chains). For our purposes the citation is a standard-of-care acknowledgment rather than a substantive engagement.

## Options to address

1. **Minimal citation, no separate discussion.** Add the paper to the list of task-based automation models already referenced (Acemoglu–Restrepo, Autor–Thompson, Acemoglu 2025, Aghion–Bunel, etc.) in one bibliography line. No body-text paragraph beyond standard lit-review mention.
2. **Citation + one-sentence contrast.** Same as Option 1 plus a single sentence noting that the mechanism there (task complementarities at the production-function level) differs from the chaining/handoff mechanism here.

## Effort vs. payoff

| Option | Effort | Payoff |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Minimal cite | Trivial (10 min) | Low — but avoided-cost of future collegial friction is non-zero |
| 2. Cite + one sentence | Trivial (30 min) | Low-medium — slightly clearer for readers, same cost |

**Recommendation:** Option 2. Marginal extra effort, gives the reader the right mental model of how the two papers differ.
