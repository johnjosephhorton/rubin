\section{Optimization}

In this section we study the firm's AI deployment problem: choosing how to integrate the AI technology into its workflow, that is, choosing the AI strategy $\T$, so as to minimize the cost of production in \eqref{eq:totalcost_shortterm}.
The broader long-run problem, in which the firm additionally redesigns jobs by bundling tasks into roles and trading specialization against coordination costs, is developed in Appendix~\ref{app:jobdesign}.



\subsection{Optimal AI Deployment}
\label{sec:optimization.short}

The firm chooses the AI strategy $\T$ for its $m$ steps $\mathcal{S} = (s_1, \dotsc, s_m)$ to minimize the total production cost $\sum_{T_b \in \T} \skillcost{b}\,\timecost{b}$ in \eqref{eq:totalcost_shortterm}.
Although the number of possible strategies grows exponentially in $m$, the cost-optimal strategy can be calculated via dynamic programming in $O(m^2)$ time.

\begin{proposition}
\label{prop:shortrun_dp}
Given a workflow with $m$ steps, the cost-minimizing AI strategy can be calculated in time $O(m^2)$ via dynamic programming.
\end{proposition}

The proof, a dynamic program over prefixes of the step sequence, is given in Appendix~\ref{app:omitted_proofs}.
